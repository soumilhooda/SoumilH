Soumil Hooda

Silicon Engineer at Google

I am a 2025 graduate of BITS Pilani, Hyderabad, where I completed a Bachelor of Engineering in Electrical and Electronics and a Master of Science in Physics. During my time there, I had the opportunity to intern at the Space Applications Centre (SAC), ISRO, and CSRI-CEERI, Pilani. I was fortunate to collaborate with wonderful researchers including Prof. Manik Gupta, Prof. Rajesh Tripathy, and Dr. Satya Prakash Ojha. My research during this period focused on applied machine learning across various domains, including remote sensing, atmospheric modelling, healthcare, and Brain-Computer Interface (BCI) systems.

I also devoted time to developing a comprehensive weather risk management system for India. I believe the Indian market in this sector is significantly underserved, which led me to develop multiple financial products such as temperature-based derivative contracts, temperature-energy quanto contracts, rainfall-agriculture quanto contracts, and wind/solar energy swaps. I also actively researched securitization for government-backed insurance schemes like the PMFBY and WBCIS. Please click here if you wish to read a more personal anecdote about this.

Most recently, my research has focused on developing a GNN-based framework for the formal verification of complex Finite State Machines (FSMs), employing RL-driven coverage analysis to enhance fault detection. This was a side project I picked up during my internship at Texas Instruments, where I developed an assertion-based verification IP for an internal communication protocol. I concluded my undergraduate program with an internship at Google, where I contributed to chassis platform development.
